Mission

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where:

Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment.
Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner.
Administrators and staff, as stewards of the institution, ensure an enabling and supportive environment for effective learning to happen.
Family, community, and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share responsibility for developing life-long learners.

History


History of San Mateo Elementary School

San Mateo Elementary School was established in 1952 in a parcel of residential land in Brgy. San Mateo, San Pablo City. It has an area of 2200 square meters. It was donated by Mr. Nicasio B. Tolentino made last January 31st 1991 with the City Government of San Pablo City, Laguna represented by its Mayor Zacarias A. Ticzon. The school has Gonzales Type building with two rooms at the first time of establishment.

In 1967, Marcos Type Building with three rooms was constructed as an additional. Another building named RP-US with three rooms was built in the late 80's. The school covered court was constructed through the help of Congressman Florante "Boy" Aquino in March 1992. In November 1994, Amante building with two rooms was added through the effort of Parent-Teacher Community Associaton (PTCA). The school gate and fence were concreted for the security and protection of every stakeholder.

It was July 2014 when Typhoon Glenda hit the school and destructed the Gonzales and RP-US Building. The first was totally condemned and replaced by Mayor Loreto S. Amante Building under the Special Education Fund. In 2018, the RP-US Building was replaced by a three-storey building under DPWH and will be used this SY 2019-2020. Through the collaborative efforts, it was made possible which led the way of delivering better services to school aged children in the community.

There are thirteen (13) classrooms comprising of a two-storey-six-classroom, one (1) two-storey-two-classroom in Amante 1 Building, three (3) in Marcos Type Building and two (2) classroom in Amante 2 Building. Its construction became possible through the efforts of Mayor Loreto S. Amante, DPWH and Department of Education.

The school has one computer laboratory with six (6) personal computer units for general use, an internet connection, a science laboratory and a library to be organized and established by present administration headed by the school head, Lady Lourence S. Alimagno.

With a highly qualified teaching staff of eight (8) professionals coming from th DepEd supportive Stakeholders, Brgy. Chairman Rodelo Arceo and Officials and with its PTA President Vilma E. Alcantara and Officers at the helm, the school's dream, mission and vision embodied to produce twenty first leaners, who are equipped, more competent and lifelong learners may soon be realized.
